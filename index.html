About the maker

I’ve always been drawn to the texture, color, and quiet weight of rocks— especially those shaped by the Great Lakes. Rock tacks started as a small idea: what if everyday thumbtacks could feel like tiny pieces of shoreline?

Each set is designed with care, from choosing stones that feel good in your hand to mounting them securely on sturdy tacks. My goal is simple: to bring a little bit of lake calm to your wall, desk, or bulletin board.

Great Lakes collection

Every lake has its own personality—color, mood, and shoreline. I highlight those differences in each collection, so you can choose the lake that feels most like home.

How the rock tacks are made

Each tack goes through a simple but careful process to make sure it looks good and holds up to everyday use.

1. Selecting stones

I choose stones for texture, color, and feel—small enough for a tack, but large enough to show off their character.

2. Cleaning & finishing

Stones are cleaned and, when needed, lightly polished to bring out natural patterns while keeping their organic look.

3. Mounting

Each stone is securely attached to a sturdy thumbtack base using strong adhesive and time to cure fully.

4. Testing & pairing

I test each tack for stability, then group them into sets that feel visually balanced and satisfying together.
